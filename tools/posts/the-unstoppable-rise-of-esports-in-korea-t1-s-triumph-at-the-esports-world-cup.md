# The Unstoppable Rise of Esports in Korea: T1's Triumph at the eSports World Cup

South Korea has long been recognized as the birthplace of esports, a nation where competitive gaming has evolved into a cultural phenomenon. With its roots in the late 1990s, the esports industry in Korea has grown exponentially, driven by a passionate fanbase and a highly skilled pool of players. Today, Korea stands at the forefront of the global esports market, continually setting benchmarks in both player performance and market development.

In 2024, the esports market in Korea is projected to generate a revenue of $297.2 million, with an anticipated annual growth rate (CAGR 2024-2028) of 6.95%, reaching a projected market volume of $388.8 million by 2028. A significant contributor to this market is esports betting, which alone is expected to garner $151.7 million in 2024. The United States may generate the highest revenue globally, but Korea remains a pivotal player with its deep-rooted esports culture and infrastructure.

**T1: A Symbol of Korean Esports Dominance**

The recent victory of T1 at the eSports World Cup is a testament to Korea's continued dominance in the esports arena. T1, renowned as the world’s top League of Legends (LoL) esports team, secured their place in history by defeating China's Top eSports (TES) with a 3:1 victory in the finals. This victory marks T1's status as the first champion of the eSports World Cup, further cementing their legacy.

Formed as a joint venture between SK Square and Comcast in 2019, T1 has rapidly ascended to the pinnacle of esports. SK Square holds a 55.4% stake in T1, while Comcast holds 34.3%. This strategic partnership has facilitated significant investments in the team, including a capital infusion of 13.5 billion won ($10.4 million) last year.

T1's financial growth is notable. Forbes valued the team at approximately $220 million in May last year, the highest among Korean sports clubs. The team recorded revenues of 23.9 billion won in 2022, reflecting a 34% average annual growth. This growth is bolstered by T1's diverse revenue streams, including the T1 Membership platform, the T1 Cafe & Arena, and the T1 Basecamp cafe, which serve as hubs for fan engagement and gaming culture.

The star power of players like Faker (Lee Sang-hyuk) has also been instrumental in T1's success. Faker's contract extension for three more years in 2022 underscores the team's commitment to maintaining a strong roster. T1's performance at the 2023 League of Legends World Championship, which attracted over 400 million cumulative viewers, showcases the team's global appeal and strategic gameplay.

**Key Insights for Businesses**

The growth trajectory of esports in Korea presents significant opportunities for businesses. Here are key insights for stakeholders looking to tap into this burgeoning market:

> 1. Investment in Infrastructure: The success of T1 highlights the importance of robust infrastructure and strategic partnerships. Businesses should consider investing in training facilities, gaming cafes, and arenas to foster talent and engage fans.

> 2. Diverse Revenue Streams: T1's approach to diversifying its revenue through membership platforms and cultural spaces offers a blueprint for sustainable growth. Businesses should explore similar models to create multiple touchpoints with their audience.

> 3. Fan Engagement: The passionate fanbase of esports presents a unique opportunity for brands to engage with a dedicated audience. Interactive platforms, exclusive content, and fan events can enhance brand loyalty and visibility.

> 4. Leveraging Star Power: Esports celebrities like Faker have significant influence. Collaborating with top players for endorsements and promotions can amplify a brand's reach and credibility within the gaming community.

> 5. Global Reach: The global viewership of events like the League of Legends World Championship underscores the international appeal of esports. Businesses should consider strategies to engage both local and global audiences.

**Conclusion**

South Korea's esports industry continues to set global standards, driven by iconic teams like T1 and a vibrant community of fans and players. The victory of T1 at the eSports World Cup is not just a triumph for the team but a testament to Korea's enduring influence in the world of competitive gaming. For businesses, the esports market offers a dynamic and rapidly growing landscape ripe with opportunities for investment, engagement, and innovation.
