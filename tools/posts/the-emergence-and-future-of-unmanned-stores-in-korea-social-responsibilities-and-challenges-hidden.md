# The Emergence and Future of Unmanned Stores in Korea: Social Responsibilities and Challenges Hidden Behind Convenience

#### What Are Unmanned Stores?

Unmanned stores are a type of retail operation that combines digital technology and automated systems to reduce labor costs and maximize efficiency. While the absence of staff makes these stores vulnerable to theft, surveillance via CCTV and the integration of artificial intelligence and automated payment systems help simplify the purchasing process. Up until the 2000s, unmanned retail primarily meant ATMs or vending machines, but with the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, these stores became increasingly popular as a way to minimize human contact. Today, there are approximately 6,300 unmanned stores operating throughout South Korea.

#### The Spread and Challenges of Unmanned Stores

The expansion of unmanned stores offers many benefits to both consumers and business owners. They can operate 24/7, reduce initial setup and labor costs, and are advantageous for small business owners. Additionally, rising numbers of single-person households and an increasing desire for efficiency have driven consumer demand for these stores.

However, despite the convenience they offer, unmanned stores also present several challenges. Between March 2021 and June 2022, there were 6,344 reported theft incidents in unmanned stores, with 52% involving minors. Standard security measures like CCTV are insufficient for prevention, and young people often copy such crimes as trends on social media. Furthermore, the lack of age verification makes it easy for minors to purchase alcohol or cigarettes. Errors in transactions pose another issue; if a mistake occurs, it is difficult to discern whether it was accidental or intentional, potentially leading to theft charges, which can be burdensome and result in wasted resources. Therefore, a discussion around the safety and social responsibilities of unmanned stores is essential.

#### Future Directions for Unmanned Stores

The future of unmanned stores is promising as technology advances, potentially enhancing consumer convenience and increasing business profitability. For instance, Amazon Go in the United States has introduced a system where customers can pick up items and leave, with the payment being processed automatically, revolutionizing the shopping experience. This technology could also be adopted in South Korea, improving both the efficiency and safety of unmanned stores. However, just as important as technological advancement is the balance between regulatory measures and consumer trust. For unmanned stores to achieve sustainable growth, it is crucial to balance technological innovation with social responsibility and regulation.
