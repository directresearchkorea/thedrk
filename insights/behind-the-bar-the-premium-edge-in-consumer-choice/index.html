# Behind the Bar: The Premium Edge in Consumer Choice

In recent years, the alcohol market has undergone significant changes. People are exploring a wider variety of alcoholic beverages, creating interesting consumption trends. These changes span from traditional favorites like beer, wine, and whiskey to newer types like craft beer and artisanal whiskey. Consumers of varying ages and backgrounds are selecting beverages that suit their unique tastes, which in turn shapes new consumption trends within the market.

Differences in Alcohol Choices Based on Income Level

As the world of alcohol becomes more diverse, we can observe certain patterns in consumer choices. Our analysis shows that there are clear differences in brand preferences among different income levels. Consumers in the lower to middle income bracket tend to consume relatively affordable alcohols such as Jim Beam, Jack Daniel's, Johnnie Walker, and Ballantine's, whereas high-income consumers prefer premium brands like Macallan, Casanoble, Dom Pérignon, and Hennessy.

Changing Alcohol Choices Based on Occasions.

However, these differences are not absolute and tend to vary depending on the situation and purpose. In fact, alcohol brand choices across different income levels show that these preferences can transcend economic capabilities. For example, even high-income individuals might choose a range of brands like Jack Daniel's, Ballantine's, and Balvenie, which vary in price.

Alcohol consumption changes significantly depending on the specific circumstances or purposes. Our study indicates that in family gatherings, beer (40%), wine (30%), and whiskey (30%) are evenly consumed, while in business meetings, champagne (40%) and cognac (60%, Hennessy 30%, Casanoble 30%) are the preferred choices. In social gatherings with friends, craft beer (50%) and whiskey (50%, Jim Beam 25%, Johnnie Walker 25%) are popular. This indicates that the choice of alcohol brand does not always correlate directly with income level.

The choice of alcohol varies in each situation, showing that consumer selections are not merely based on personal preference but are also influenced by the ambiance and purpose of the occasion. For example, celebratory events often feature champagne or wine, which enhance social interactions and suit the festive atmosphere.

Deep Understanding of Alcohol Consumption Patterns

Understanding alcohol consumption patterns goes beyond simply analyzing consumers' economic capabilities; it involves a comprehensive understanding of their lifestyles, social contexts, and purposes. This article deepens our understanding of the diversity and complexity of the alcohol market, providing crucial insights for delivering products that meet each consumer's needs. Alcohol choices reflect more than individual tastes; they play a significant role across all aspects of life.
